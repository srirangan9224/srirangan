## Overview

As a member of the **Georgia Tech Solar Racing (GTSR)** Auxiliary team, I contributed to the design and implementation of the electrical systems for our solar-powered vehicle. I worked within a specialized team to develop the lighting infrastructure, ensuring that every signaling component—from safety strobes to high-visibility headlamps—met rigorous racing standards and automotive requirements.

## Key Features

- **Full-Suite Lighting Design**: Collaborated on the design and layout of PCBs for the entire vehicle lighting spectrum, including **headlights, rear lights, side indicators, and emergency strobe systems**.
- **End-to-End KiCad Workflow**: Managed the complete development cycle within **KiCad**, from initial schematic capture to the final PCB layout, ensuring precise trace routing for power-heavy LED components.
- **Empirical Brightness Validation**: Performed functional testing using a standardized **test light** to validate luminosity. I compared the PCB output against reference brightness levels to ensure compliance with road safety and race regulations.
- **System Integration**: Focused on the seamless integration of these auxiliary boards into the vehicle’s broader electrical architecture, ensuring reliable power delivery across all lighting modules.

## Technologies Used

- **KiCad EDA**: The primary tool used for both the schematic design and the physical PCB layout of the lighting controllers.
- **Hardware Bench Testing**: Utilized manual test lights and comparative analysis to verify the performance and duty cycles of the signaling hardware.
- **Automotive Electronics**: Applied principles of thermal management and current regulation to ensure the longevity of high-intensity LEDs under continuous racing conditions.

## Challenges and Learnings

A major challenge was the **high-density layout** required for the strobe and side lighting. Because these boards had to fit into specific aerodynamic housings, I had to optimize the KiCad layout to minimize footprint without compromising the heat dissipation needed for the high-output LEDs.

Through the validation process, I learned the value of **comparative testing**. Using a test light provided immediate, practical feedback on whether our driver circuits were delivering enough current to match the required brightness levels, a crucial step in ensuring the vehicle remained visible and safe on the track.

## Outcome

The auxiliary team successfully delivered a robust, integrated lighting system that passed all pre-race inspections. This project solidified my proficiency in **KiCad** and provided invaluable experience in working within a multidisciplinary team to solve complex, real-world electrical integration challenges in the automotive space.
